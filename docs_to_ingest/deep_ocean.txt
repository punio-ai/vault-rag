The deep ocean, defined as depths below 200 meters, remains one of the least explored environments on Earth. Over 80% of the ocean floor has never been mapped with modern sonar technology, and scientists estimate that millions of marine species have yet to be discovered. Extreme conditions including near-freezing temperatures, crushing pressure, and complete darkness make exploration technically challenging and expensive.

Advances in autonomous underwater vehicles (AUVs) and deep-sea submersibles are gradually opening this frontier. In 2019, the vessel Limiting Factor completed a full dive to the bottom of the Mariana Trench's Challenger Deep, reaching nearly 11,000 meters. These missions reveal ecosystems thriving around hydrothermal vents, where chemosynthetic bacteria form the base of food chains entirely independent of sunlight, challenging our understanding of where life can exist.
