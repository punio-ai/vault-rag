Renewable energy sources like solar and wind power have seen dramatic cost reductions over the past decade. Solar photovoltaic modules are now over 90% cheaper than they were in 2010, making solar energy competitive with fossil fuels in many regions. This shift has led to massive deployments worldwide, with China, the United States, and India leading new installations.

Wind energy has followed a similar trajectory, particularly offshore wind farms which can harness stronger and more consistent winds over the ocean. Modern turbines can generate over 15 megawatts each, enough to power thousands of homes. The challenge remains in energy storage and grid integration, as renewable sources are intermittent by nature and require backup systems or large-scale battery solutions to maintain stable power supply.
